Frank Fritz Biography

Frank Fritz is an American television personality and antique collector best known for his role on the History Channel's "American Pickers." Born on October 11, 1965, in Davenport, Iowa, Frank developed a passion for collecting antiques at a young age. He has built a reputation for his keen eye for valuable items and his extensive knowledge of American history.

Early Life and Career

Frank Fritz grew up in a small town where he developed an interest in antiques and collectibles. His fascination with history led him to explore various flea markets and antique shops. After graduating high school, Frank began working as a firefighter while pursuing his passion for collecting on the side.

In the early 1990s, Frank decided to take a leap of faith and turn his hobby into a full-time career. He opened an antique shop in Iowa, which allowed him to connect with fellow collectors and sellers. His expertise and enthusiasm quickly caught the attention of producers looking for hosts for a new reality show focused on antique collecting.

Launch of American Pickers

"American Pickers" premiered on the History Channel in January 2010, featuring Frank Fritz and Mike Wolfe as they traveled across the United States in search of hidden treasures. The show's unique premise, along with the engaging personalities of its hosts, quickly garnered a loyal following.

Frank's expertise in antiques and his charismatic demeanor made him a fan favorite. Viewers were drawn to his ability to uncover the stories behind the items they discovered, adding a deeper layer of interest to the show.

Biography of Frank Fritz

Frank Fritz was born on October 11, 1965, in Davenport, Iowa. He developed an interest in antiques and collectibles at a young age, which eventually led him to pursue a career in this field. Frank's enthusiasm for treasure hunting was evident as he spent countless hours scouring flea markets and garage sales, searching for unique items with historical significance.

Early Life and Background

Growing up in Iowa, Frank was influenced by the rich history and culture of the Midwest. His father worked as a businessman, which instilled in him the value of hard work and determination. As a child, Frank would often accompany his father to local auctions, where he first discovered his passion for antiques.

In addition to his interest in collecting, Frank also developed a love for restoring old items. He would spend hours tinkering with various antiques, learning the art of restoration and preservation. This hands-on experience laid the foundation for his future career in the antique industry.

Education

Frank attended local schools in Iowa and later graduated from high school. He did not pursue higher education in a traditional sense; instead, he chose to immerse himself in the world of antiques. His practical experience and self-education in the field have made him a knowledgeable expert.

Career on American Pickers

Frank Fritz's career took a significant turn when he was approached to co-host American Pickers alongside Mike Wolfe. The show premiered on the History Channel in 2010 and quickly gained popularity due to its unique concept of treasure hunting across America.

As a co-host, Frank's role involves traveling to various locations, meeting collectors, and negotiating deals for unique antiques. His charismatic personality and expertise in antiques have made him a beloved figure among fans of the show. Together with Mike Wolfe, Frank has explored countless hidden treasures, shedding light on the stories behind each item.

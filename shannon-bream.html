Biography of Shannon Bream

Shannon Bream was born on December 23, 1970, in Tallahassee, Florida. She grew up in a family that valued education and encouraged her to pursue her interests. From a young age, Shannon displayed a keen interest in public speaking and debate, which laid the foundation for her future career in journalism.

She attended Liberty University, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in Business and a minor in Political Science. Following her graduation, she pursued a career in law and graduated from the Florida State University College of Law. However, her passion for journalism ultimately led her to switch paths, and she began her career in television news.

Career Highlights

Shannon Bream began her journalism career at a local news station in Florida before moving on to larger markets. She gained significant recognition while working at NBC affiliate stations in Florida and later joined the Washington D.C. bureau of Fox News Channel in 2007. Her reporting covered a wide range of topics, with a particular focus on political news and major national events.

One of her significant career milestones came when she was named the anchor of "Fox News @ Night," where she brought her unique perspective and expertise to the coverage of breaking news and political analysis.

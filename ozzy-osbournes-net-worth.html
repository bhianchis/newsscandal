Biography of Ozzy Osbourne

Born John Michael Osbourne on December 3, 1948, in Birmingham, England, Ozzy Osbourne grew up in a working-class family. He faced a challenging upbringing, which shaped his rebellious persona. His passion for music led him to form the band Black Sabbath in 1968, which ultimately became one of the pioneers of heavy metal music.

Early Career and Rise to Fame

Ozzy's career began to take off in the early 1970s with the release of Black Sabbath's self-titled debut album. The band's unique sound and dark themes resonated with audiences, leading to a string of successful albums, including "Paranoid," "Master of Reality," and "Sabbath Bloody Sabbath." Their music helped define the heavy metal genre and established Ozzy as a prominent figure in the industry.

Key Milestones in Black Sabbath's Career

  • Formation of Black Sabbath in 1968
  • Release of "Paranoid" in 1970, which became one of their most iconic albums
  • Induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2006

Ozzy Osbourne's Solo Career

After being dismissed from Black Sabbath in 1979, Ozzy embarked on a solo career that would further solidify his status as a rock icon. His debut solo album, "Blizzard of Ozz," released in 1980, featured hits like "Crazy Train" and "Mr. Crowley." This success paved the way for a series of successful albums throughout the 1980s and 1990s, including "Diary of a Madman" and "No More Tears."

Notable Solo Albums

  • "Blizzard of Ozz" (1980)
  • "Diary of a Madman" (1981)
  • "No More Tears" (1991)

Business Ventures and Endorsements

Early Life and Background

Frank Fritz was born on October 11, 1965, in Davenport, Iowa. Growing up in a Midwest town, Frank developed a keen interest in collecting antiques and memorabilia from a young age. His passion for history and vintage items was nurtured by his family, who often took him to flea markets and garage sales.

As a child, Frank was fascinated by the stories behind each item he encountered, and this curiosity laid the groundwork for his future career. He began collecting various items, from old toys to vintage advertising signs, honing his skills as a picker long before he became a household name.

Career Beginnings

Before embarking on his journey as a professional picker, Frank worked in various jobs, including being a firefighter and a restoration expert. His experience in restoring vintage items provided him with valuable skills that he would later apply to his picking endeavors.

Frank's big break came when he met Mike Wolfe, another passionate picker, who shared his love for antiques. Together, they founded a business that focused on buying and reselling unique items, setting the stage for their future success on television.

Rise to Fame on American Pickers

In 2010, Frank Fritz's life took a dramatic turn when he became a co-host on the popular reality TV series "American Pickers." The show follows Frank and Mike as they travel across the United States, searching for hidden treasures in barns, attics, and yards.

The show's engaging format, combined with Frank's charismatic personality, quickly garnered a loyal fanbase. Viewers were drawn to his genuine enthusiasm for antiques and his ability to uncover the stories behind each item he discovered.

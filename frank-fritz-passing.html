Biography of Frank Fritz

Frank Fritz was born on October 11, 1965, in Davenport, Iowa. He gained prominence as a co-host of the hit television series "American Pickers," which premiered on the History Channel in 2010. Along with his partner Mike Wolfe, Frank traveled across the United States, searching for hidden treasures and unique antiques. His passion for collecting and his engaging personality made him a fan favorite.

Early Life

Frank Fritz grew up in a modest household, where he developed an early interest in antiques and collectibles. His father worked as a mechanic, while his mother was a homemaker. From a young age, Frank was fascinated by the stories behind old items and the history they held. This passion would later shape his career and lead him to become a renowned antique collector.

After completing high school, Frank pursued a career as a firefighter, but his love for collecting never waned. He often spent his free time attending flea markets, garage sales, and estate sales, where he would search for valuable antiques. This hobby eventually turned into a full-time career when he partnered with Mike Wolfe to create "American Pickers."

Frank Fritz was born on October 11, 1965, in Davenport, Iowa. He developed an early passion for antiques and collecting, which would shape his future career. After graduating from high school, Frank worked various jobs before diving into the world of antique picking.

Growing up in the Midwest, Frank was surrounded by a rich history of Americana. Career Highlights

Frank's career as an antique picker began in the early 2000s when he partnered with Mike Wolfe to create the concept for American Pickers. The show premiered on the History Channel in 2010 and quickly gained popularity for its entertaining and informative content.

Rise to Fame

American Pickers showcased Frank and Mike's adventures as they traveled across the United States, searching for valuable items in people's homes and barns. Their chemistry and genuine passion for antiques resonated with viewers, leading to a devoted fanbase. The show highlighted the importance of preserving history through collectibles and encouraged viewers to appreciate the stories behind their belongings.

Achievements and Recognition

Throughout his career, Frank received numerous accolades for his contributions to the antiques community. He became a respected figure among collectors and was known for his keen eye for valuable items. His expertise and dedication to the craft set him apart in the industry.

American Pickers: The Show

Viewer Engagement

Growth of the Antiques Market
